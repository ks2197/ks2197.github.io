Projects
Deep Reinforcement Learning for SMARTGRID Control

Currently, I am working under the supervision of Prof. Andreas J. Kassler within his research group at the Intelligent Systems and Networks Lab at Deggendorf Institute of Technology, Germany, where I focus on developing and optimizing energy-sharing models using multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) to enhance smart grid efficiency. My research primarily revolves around designing and implementing advanced reward functions in a multi-agent deep deterministic policy gradient (MADDPG) environment to optimize vehicle-to-grid (V2G) and grid-to-vehicle (G2V) energy flows. This optimization enables efficient energy distribution between smart buildings and electric vehicles, fostering sustainable energy management within modern grids.

In addition to reinforcement learning-based optimization, I have worked on developing community-driven energy-sharing metrics, such as shared energy and community contribution indices. By leveraging proximity-based collaboration, these metrics help create a more resilient energy-sharing ecosystem by maximizing energy efficiency and minimizing dependence on the main power grid. The implementation of such dynamic metrics enhances the ability of energy prosumers to coordinate, distribute, and utilize renewable energy resources effectively, reducing overall energy wastage and promoting sustainable energy transitions.

To validate and scale these models, I have conducted simulations in real-world-inspired environments using Python-based frameworks like CityLearn and custom MARL agents. Through these simulations, I have demonstrated significant improvements in energy utilization, cost reduction, and grid load balancing, proving the feasibility of these models in large-scale smart grid applications. These advancements contribute towards scalable and adaptive energy management systems, making intelligent grids more efficient, cost-effective, and environmentally sustainable.


GW Skymap
I have worked with the Mathematical Physics of Gravitational Waves (GW) research group within the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Western University, Ontario, Canada, under the supervision of Prof. Shree Ram Valluri. My work focused on developing gravitational wave visualization software, enabling the efficient analysis of LIGO, Virgo, and KAGRA detections. As part of this project, I created a user-friendly GUI to interact with the LIGO GraceDB REST API, allowing seamless visualization of thousands of single-order and multi-order gravitational wave events in the HEALPix data format. This tool provides an intuitive interface for researchers to explore and interpret gravitational wave data effectively.

Beyond software development, I designed and implemented 2D and 3D visualization features to enhance the representation of gravitational wave source locations. These visualizations include 2D projected contours and 3D scatter plots, offering an interactive and comprehensive view of detected gravitational wave events. The integration of such features helps in better understanding event distributions and patterns across the sky, aiding researchers in analyzing astrophysical phenomena with greater precision.

To ensure robustness and usability, I extensively tested and integrated HEALPix-based visualizations within the system. This involved validating functionalities such as Mollweide Projection and 3D scatter views, ensuring compatibility with existing astronomical data formats. Additionally, my work contributed to the integration of the Gravitational Wave Sky Map Project into the open-source Stellarium platform, making gravitational wave data more accessible to the broader scientific community and astronomy enthusiasts alike.
