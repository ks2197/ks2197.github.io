Projects

Deep Reinforcement Learning for SMARTGRID Control

Currently, I am working under the supervision of Prof. Andreas J. Kassler at the Intelligent Systems and Networks Lab (Deggendorf Institute of Technology, Germany). My research involves developing and optimizing energy-sharing models using Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) to enhance smart grid efficiency.

My work includes designing advanced reward functions in a MADDPG environment to optimize Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) and Grid-to-Vehicle (G2V) energy flows, fostering sustainable energy management in smart grids.

I have conducted simulations using Python-based frameworks like CityLearn and custom MARL agents to demonstrate improvements in energy utilization, cost reduction, and grid load balancing.

GW Skymap

I worked with the Mathematical Physics of Gravitational Waves (GW) research group at Western University, Ontario, Canada, under the supervision of Prof. Shree Ram Valluri.

I developed a gravitational wave visualization tool for analyzing LIGO, Virgo, and KAGRA detections. My work involved designing a user-friendly GUI that integrates with the LIGO GraceDB REST API to visualize gravitational wave events in HEALPix format.

I implemented 2D and 3D visualization features, including Mollweide Projections and 3D scatter plots, and contributed to integrating the Gravitational Wave Sky Map Project into Stellarium.
